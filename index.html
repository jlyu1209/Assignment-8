REDUCING WASTE


We are all connected. Waste contaminates the air we breathe, the water we drink, the soil we use to grow food and introduces harmful substance to the environment. It can lead to serious problems not just to humans but other species and make us more susceptible to harmful diseases. Humans create the most waste and we’re the only ones that can undo it.

WAYS TO REDUCE WASTE



HOW CAN WE GO GREEN IN EVERDAY LIFE?

REDUCE

The best way to reduce waste is to not create it in the first place. Here are some simple daily tipes to reduce waste.

  • Purchase your products more wisely. Look out for products with less packaging. You can also avoid packaging by buying in bulk.
  • Buy less or not at all. Say no to trends and think twice. A lot of the times, things we buy may not be things we actually need.
  • Reduce food waste. Meal plan in advance to avoid overbuying. Store your left-over and eat it the next day. Freeze your goods.


REUSE

Reusing an item saves about 20 times as much energy as recycling it. So many of the things we use can be used more than once.

  • Avoid single use items. These things include things such as plastic straws, plates, and cups. Get in the habbit of carrying around your own reusable water bottle or eco-bag.
  • Save your budget and consider buying second-hand. You may be surprised by the variety and how well maintained things are.
  • Buy items that contain recycled material.


RECYCLE

When U.S. recycling level reaches 75%, it will be equivalent of removing 55 million cars from the U.S. roads each year and generate 1.5 million new jobs. Yet only 21.4% of Americans recycle properly. The public is still confused about what items are recyclable.

  • Paper, cardboard, glass bottles and jars, metal containers, and plastic products are all be recyclable.
  • Make sure to wash items so there are no food waste on it. It can’t be recycled if it’s contaminated.
  • Do not put recyclables in plastic bag. It can’t be recycled inside a plastic bag. Opt out for an open paper bag instead.


AND COMPOST

30% of the things we throw away are compostable. Compost is organic material that can be added to soil to help plants grow.

  • Rule of thumb: Anything grown naturally from ground can be compostable. This means cooked or processed foods are not.
  • Your compost pile should have an equal amount of browns to greens. Browns include materials such as dead leaves, branches, and twigs. Green materials include items such as coffee grounds, clippings, vegetable waste, and fruit scraps.
  • Store your compost in dry and cool area. Moisture is key to composting so wet your compost with water from time to time.
